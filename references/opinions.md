# Opinions — how to build an opinions profile

This is a **template**. For each new client, collect their real, specific
opinions about their industry. Deploy one per page where it makes the copy
feel human instead of generic.

A genuine opinion is the clearest line between a real operator's voice and
AI content. AI hedges; a real tradesperson, doctor or founder has takes.

---

## How to build it

Interview the client (or have the user relay) and capture opinions in these
buckets. Adapt the bucket names to the niche.

### On the industry
What the client thinks is wrong with how their industry usually operates —
and what they do differently. Often the reason the business exists.

### On what customers should actually do
The honest advice the client gives even when it costs them a job — the
DIY fix, the cheaper option, the "you don't need us for this".

### On the product / service decisions
Strong, defensible positions on the choices customers face — which option
is better and why, what's overrated, what's worth paying for.

### On pricing
How the client thinks pricing should work, and the practices in their
industry they consider dishonest.

### On the common situation
The niche's recurring scenario and the client's firm view on handling it.

### On what a good operator never does
The line the client won't cross — the small professional standards that,
stated plainly, build trust.

---

## How to use opinions in content

- **One strong opinion per page, max.** Deploy it where the page would
  otherwise feel generic.
- **Always back an opinion with a number or a story.** "X is better" is a
  bumper sticker. "X costs $800/year, Y costs $150, 4-year payback" is an
  opinion. Pair with [`stories.md`](stories.md) and the stats file.
- **Tell people when NOT to hire the business.** The single biggest voice
  tell. The client should be willing to talk a customer out of a paid job
  when a free fix exists. Generic AI never does this.
- **Keep opinions defensible.** Specific, grounded, and something the
  client would actually say to a customer's face — not a hot take for
  effect.

---

## Worked example — Plumbing Co

> One opinion per blog post, backed by a number.

- **On the industry:** franchise operators train upselling, not plumbing —
  it's why Plumbing Co is owner-operated and flat-fee.
- **On what customers should do:** try the kettle first on a blocked
  kitchen sink; it clears about half the jobs. Unscrew the aerator before
  calling about low pressure — 30 seconds, fixes it nine times out of ten.
- **On product decisions:** electric storage hot water is a bad financial
  call past 3 years — ~$800/yr to run vs ~$150 for a heat pump, ~4-year
  payback.
- **On pricing:** any price in writing before work starts; call-out fees
  credited against the job or waived; "starting at" pricing is a red flag.
- **On what a good plumber never does:** leave a mess, start work before
  the price is approved, or recommend replacement when a repair will do.
